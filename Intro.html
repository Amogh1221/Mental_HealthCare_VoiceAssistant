Mental HealthCare Voice Assistant

"An AI-powered psychiatric assessment system that conducts empathetic clinical interviews through natural voice interactions, analyzes conversation patterns using dual-LLM architecture, and provides evidence-based psychological insights grounded in clinical psychopathology literature."

Problem Statement

Mental health services face significant accessibility barriers including limited availability of psychiatrists, high costs, stigma, and long waiting times. Many individuals struggle to access timely mental health support, leading to untreated conditions and worsening symptoms. There is a critical need for an accessible, stigma-free preliminary assessment tool that can provide immediate support while encouraging professional intervention when necessary.

Literature Review / Market Research

Existing chatbots like Woebot and Wysa provide mental health support but lack voice interaction and clinical depth. Research shows voice-based interfaces improve engagement and empathy perception. Clinical frameworks from "Sims' Symptoms in the Mind" provide validated symptom taxonomies for accurate pattern recognition.

Research Gap / Innovation

Unlike existing solutions, our system combines: (1) Natural voice-based interaction with speech recognition and synthesis, (2) Dual-LLM architecture separating conversation from clinical analysis, (3) RAG-enhanced responses grounded in 122k+ clinical Q&A pairs from psychiatric literature, (4) Dynamic pattern analysis across 6 psychological domains with evidence-based recommendations.

System Methodology

Dataset / Input

Compumacy/Psych_data: 122,324 psychiatric Q&A pairs extracted from "Sims' Symptoms in the Mind". Embedded using sentence-transformers/all-MiniLM-L6-v2 and stored in ChromaDB vector database. Voice input processed via Web Speech API with real-time transcription.

Model / Architecture

LLM1 (Llama3.2:3b): Conducts psychiatric interviews, decides CONTINUE/ANALYZE. LLM2 (Nemotron-mini): Analyzes patterns across emotional, cognitive, behavioral, interpersonal, stress, and unclear domains. RAG Engine: Retrieves top-5 relevant clinical contexts for evidence-based responses.
